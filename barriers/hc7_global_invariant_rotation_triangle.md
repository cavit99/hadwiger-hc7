# A reduced rotation triangle defeats state-local HC7 potentials

## Status

This is an explicit, verifier-backed barrier to five proposed global
invariants for the near-`K_7` state graph.  It strengthens the single-edge
involution warning: three literal rotations on one fixed five-row frame form
a reduced cycle, with no consecutive move the inverse of the preceding move.

The construction occurs in `K_2` joined with the icosahedron and persists in
a long pentagonal two-apex tube.  These hosts are not contraction-critical;
they have the permitted common fixed-pair terminal.  Accordingly the result
does not refute the desired `HC_7` exchange theorem.  It shows exactly what a
well-founded endgame must remember.

## 1. The planar tube and its two apices

For `m>=1`, let `T_m` have vertices `T,B` and five-cycles

\[
 R_r=\{v_{r,0},\ldots,v_{r,4}\}\qquad(0\le r\le m).
\]

Join `T` to every vertex of `R_0`, join `B` to every vertex of `R_m`, and,
with subscripts modulo five, add

\[
 v_{r,i}v_{r,i+1},\qquad
 v_{r,i}v_{r+1,i},\qquad
 v_{r,i}v_{r+1,i-1}.
\]

Thus `T_1` is the icosahedron.  Put

\[
                         G_m=K_2\vee T_m,
\]

and call the adjacent universal vertices `p,q`.  The companion verifier
checks planarity and five-connectivity of `T_m`, and hence
seven-connectivity of `G_m`, for the two concrete certificates `m=1,8`.
Only those two finite hosts are needed below.

Both are `K_7`-minor-free.  In a hypothetical seven-bag clique model, at
most two bags contain `p,q`; deleting them leaves five pairwise adjacent
connected bags in the planar graph `T_m`, a `K_5` minor.  Moreover

\[
                         G_m-\{p,q\}=T_m
\]

is planar.  Hence `{p,q}` is a literal common fixed-pair terminal.

## 2. One fixed frame and three states

Write `u_i=v_{0,i}` and `w_i=v_{1,i}`.  Define

\[
 C=\{u_3,u_4,w_3,w_4\},\qquad
 F_1=\{u_1,w_1\},\qquad F_2=\{u_2\}.
\]

For `m=1`, put `F_3={w_0,w_2,B}`.  For `m>=2`, put

\[
 F_3=\{w_0,w_2,B\}\cup\bigcup_{r=2}^m R_r.
\]

Finally put `F_4={p}`, `F_5={q}`.  These five bags are connected and
pairwise adjacent.  In each line below, the first displayed bag is the
deficient centre, the second is the active foreign row, and the five `F_i`
are unchanged:

\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c}
\text{state}&(\text{centre},\text{active row})&\text{unique missing row}\\
\hline
M_T&(\{T\},\{u_0\}\cup C)&F_3\\
M_C&(C,\{T,u_0\})&F_1\\
M_0&(\{u_0\},\{T\}\cup C)&F_2.
\end{array}                                                    \tag{2.1}
\]

Every row is literal and every claimed adjacency is checked by the
companion verifier.

## 3. The reduced three-rotation word

The following are exact single-gate rotations in the sense of
`results/hc7_near_k7_rotation_edge.md`:

\[
 M_T\xrightarrow{\ Z=\{u_0\}\ }M_C
 \xrightarrow{\ Z=\{T\}\ }M_0
 \xrightarrow{\ Z=C\ }M_T.                                 \tag{3.1}
\]

For example, in the first move the old active row is
`{u_0} dotcup C`; the gate `{u_0}` repairs the missing `F_3` spoke after
being joined to the old centre `{T}`, while the residual `C` becomes the
new centre and misses exactly `F_1`.  The other two checks are identical
and are performed literally by the verifier.

No consecutive move in (3.1) is the inverse of its predecessor.  The
inverse of the first move uses the same gate `{u_0}` and returns directly
from `M_C` to `M_T`, whereas (3.1) next uses `{T}` and goes to `M_0`.
Thus free cancellation of immediate inverse rotations does not remove the
cycle.

The following data are constant around (3.1):

* the five literal fixed rows `F_1,...,F_5`;
* the used vertex set;
* the active carrier union
  `C union {T,u_0}`; and
* the coherent terminal pair `{p,q}`.

The centre orders are `1,4,1`; the affected centre/row size pairs are
`(1,5),(4,2),(1,5)`.  Hence no centre-order, sorted bag-size, convex
bag-size, or lexicographic state statistic decreases on every arrow.

## 4. Consequences for candidate invariants

### 4.1 State-local lexicographic potentials: false

The old two-cycle already ruled out strict descent on every legal rotation.
The reduced triangle rules out the attempted repair “ignore the immediate
inverse and orient every remaining local handoff.”  A strictly decreasing
quantity on all three arrows would give

\[
 \Phi(M_T)>\Phi(M_C)>\Phi(M_0)>\Phi(M_T).
\]

### 4.2 Quotienting inverse rotations: insufficient

Suppressing a move followed immediately by its exact inverse leaves the
word (3.1).  Taking instead the full equivalence relation generated by
inverse rotation edges collapses all three states—and, in general, the
entire connected rotation component.  Such a quotient has no internal
rank and erases the three different missing-row duties.  It can be useful
only if the whole equivalence class receives one terminal/coherence
certificate.

### 4.3 Fixed-frame carrier unions: false

The frame and active union are literally identical in all three states,
yet their missing row cycles through `F_3,F_1,F_2`.  Therefore neither the
fixed frame nor the union of all vertices available to the current
centre/donor determines a monotone state or an attained duty.

### 4.4 Unranked local receivers: can cycle

Each arrow is a valid labelled near-model handoff.  Accepting such an arrow
merely because it changes the deficient centre or missing row permits the
three handoffs to return to their starting datum.  A local receiver is a
global outcome only after it either leaves the rotation component or
carries extra response data which cannot return unchanged.

### 4.5 Sink-SCC coherence: not falsified

All single-gate rotations are reversible, so their strongly connected
components, rather than their individual vertices, are the natural units.
The present component has exactly the desired coherent outcome: the same
literal pair `{p,q}` deletes to a `K_5`-minor-free graph, even in the
length-eight tube.  The example therefore supports, rather than refutes,
the following restricted target.

> **Closed-component coherence target.**  In the response-enriched state
> graph reachable from the atomic `HC_7` twin seam, every sink strongly
> connected component either contains a common exact-state/`K_7`/strict
> exact-seven exit, or admits one pair `{a,b}`, independent of the state,
> such that `G-{a,b}` is `K_5`-minor-free.

This is the weakest tested global formulation not defeated here.  The pair
must be existential and common to the entire component; a pair selected
separately from each model or each web page is not enough.  The state graph
must also retain the named proper-minor responses: the construction shows
that bare model rotations and fixed-frame carrier data cannot prove the
target.

## 5. The three current global proposals

### 5.1 Active packet-one shore order modulo rotations

There are two separate warnings.

First, current centre order is not a function on a rotation class.  Already
in (3.1), the active deficient piece has orders `1,4,1`, while all three
states lie in one exact rotation class.  In particular, declaring the move
from the order-four representative to either order-one representative a
“strict local decrease” does not leave the quotient class at all.  This
does not by itself identify a near-model centre with an actual packet-one
shore; it rules out precisely that proposed extension of the shore rank
through `S1` rotations.

Second, the length-eight host has literal exact-seven boundaries

\[
 S_r=\{p,q\}\cup R_r\qquad(1\le r\le7).                 \tag{5.1}
\]

The two open shores are

\[
 L_r=\{T\}\cup R_0\cup\cdots\cup R_{r-1},\qquad
 H_r=\{B\}\cup R_{r+1}\cup\cdots\cup R_8.             \tag{5.2}
\]

Each is connected and `S_r`-full.  Each has packet capacity exactly one.
Indeed, an `S_r`-full packet in `L_r` must use at least three vertices of
`R_{r-1}`: those are the only shore vertices adjacent to the five literal
vertices of `R_r`, and each sees only two of them.  Two disjoint full
packets would therefore require at least six vertices of the five-cycle
`R_{r-1}`.  The other side is symmetric.

Thus every (5.1) is an actual `(1,1)` exact-seven adhesion, but it has two
equally legitimate packet-one orientations.  Their orders are

\[
                         |L_r|=1+5r,qquad
                         |H_r|=1+5(8-r).                 \tag{5.3}
\]

Moving the frontier one ring decreases one orientation and increases the
other.  The tube is not contraction-critical and does not assert that such
a move is a legal attained-state handoff.  It does prove that order plus
the local `(1,1)` geometry cannot preserve the chosen side: a valid rank
must carry a literal nested shore identity through every neutral move.

The same warning applies to a genuine exact-seven receiver.  If `Q` is a
rotation class and its representatives carry packet-one shores of different
orders, a handoff

\[
               (M,L)\longrightarrow(M',L'),\qquad |L'|<|L|,
\]

does **not** imply

\[
 \min\{|J|:(N,J)\in Q'\}<\min\{|J|:(N,J)\in Q\}.
\]

Thus a local shore-size decrease is globally valid only if it changes the
whole rotation class and compares a class-invariant carrier, not the chosen
representative.

There is one important positive special case.  If one minimizes `|L|` over
**all actual oriented exact-seven `(1,2)` separations**, then a literal
packet-one receiver `L' proper-subset L` is impossible independently of its
returned state.  This is proved in
`results/hc7_exact7_global_thin_shore_minimality.md`.  It avoids the present
barrier because it neither quotients through `S1` rotations nor admits
`(1,1)` cells: both the old and new objects are actual, intrinsically
oriented `(1,2)` separations.

### 5.2 The coarse fixed-frame carrier `A union U`

This is exactly the invariant held constant by (3.1):

\[
                 A\cup U=C\cup\{T,u_0\}
\]

at every state.  It is a useful identifier for the neutral component, but
not a progress measure inside it.  It becomes a well-founded rank only
after proving that every nonterminal move out of the **entire** component
lands in a component whose literal carrier is a proper subset.  A move
which merely chooses a smaller centre inside the same carrier proves
nothing.

### 5.3 Newman/confluence from twin seams

The triangle is also a minimal counterexample to a confluence-only
endgame.  Regard every legal rotation as a rewrite in both directions.
Every local peak on the three states closes in one further step along the
third edge, so the system is locally confluent in the strongest possible
finite sense.  Nevertheless it has no irreducible state and admits an
infinite walk around (3.1).  Alternatively orient the triangle cyclically;
then local confluence is vacuous because every state has one successor,
but termination still fails.

Hence a twin-seam diamond does not permit Newman's lemma until termination
has been proved independently.  Even termination plus confluence would
only produce a normal-form component; a separate theorem must identify
that component as a common exact state, `K_7`, strict receiver, or coherent
fixed pair.

## 6. Exact sufficient termination scaffold

Before giving the safe form, note that merely declaring a smaller active
set on every strict edge is insufficient.  The following four-state system
is the minimal neutral-reset certificate:

\[
 x_4\leftrightarrow x_1,qquad
 y_3\leftrightarrow y_5,qquad
 x_4\longrightarrow y_3,qquad
 y_5\longrightarrow x_4.                               \tag{6.1}
\]

Subscripts are the declared active-set orders; the double arrows are
neutral equivalences.  Both strict arrows decrease their local orders,
`4>3` and `5>4`, but

\[
              x_4\longrightarrow y_3\leftrightarrow y_5
                    \longrightarrow x_4
\]

is a cycle.  Thus “every receiver is locally smaller” proves termination
only when neutral moves preserve the quantity being compared.  The literal
rotation triangle shows that such preservation cannot be assumed for
centre order or a representative-dependent shore surrogate.

The following abstract formulation is the safe rigorous repair exposed by
the falsification.

### Lemma 6.1 (termination modulo the full rotation groupoid)

Let `Sigma` be the finite set of literal states in a fixed finite host.
Partition the allowed moves into reversible neutral moves and strict
handoffs.  Let `~` be the **equivalence closure** of all neutral moves, and
write `[M]` for a class.  Suppose there is a map

\[
                    [M]\longmapsto C([M])\subseteq V(G)
\]

such that every strict handoff from a representative of `[M]` to a
representative of `[N]` satisfies

\[
                   [M]\ne[N],\qquad
                   C([N])\subsetneq C([M]).              \tag{6.2}
\]

Then the quotient transition system has no infinite strict walk.

If, in addition, strict transitions are locally confluent modulo `~`, then
they are confluent modulo `~`.  To finish the `HC_7` application one must
still prove that every sink class is terminal or has one common fixed pair.

#### Proof

Along every strict quotient edge, (6.2) strictly decreases the nonnegative
integer `|C([M])|`.  Hence there are at most `|V(G)|` strict edges in a
walk.  Neutral moves have already been collapsed into `~`, proving
termination of the quotient.  The confluence assertion is Newman's lemma
applied to this terminating quotient system.  The final sentence is a
classification obligation, not a consequence of rewriting theory.
\(\square\)

For the current proof spine, the missing work is now exact: either construct
such a class-invariant nested carrier across frame changes and exact-seven
handoffs, or prove the closed-component coherence target directly.  The
rotation triangle shows that centre order, current packet-one shore order,
and fixed-frame `A union U` do not supply (6.2) on their own.

At the purely existential level, no numerical potential is needed: the
full response-enriched state graph of a fixed finite `G` is finite, and its
SCC condensation is automatically a directed acyclic graph.  One may choose
a sink SCC.  This tautological well-foundedness merely relocates the
mathematics: one must prove that every such sink contains a terminal outcome
or one coherent fixed pair.  Lemma 6.1 is a stronger, checkable way to prove
that a proposed handoff really leaves the current SCC.
