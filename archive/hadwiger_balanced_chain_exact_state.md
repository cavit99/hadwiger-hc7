# Nested bad splits: monotone stabilization and exact-state gluing

## 1. Defect composition on a bond chain

Let \(D\) be a full shore on boundary \(S\).  A **bond chain** is a
strictly nested sequence

\[
 \varnothing\ne X_1\subsetneq X_2\subsetneq\cdots
 \subsetneq X_m\subsetneq D                            \tag{1.1}
\]

such that every \(X_i\) and \(D-X_i\) is connected.  Put

\[
 P_i=S-N_S(X_i),\qquad Q_i=S-N_S(D-X_i).             \tag{1.2}
\]

### Lemma 1.1 (monotone composition)

For \(i<j\),

\[
 P_j\subseteq P_i,\qquad Q_i\subseteq Q_j.           \tag{1.3}
\]

Consequently the sequence of defect pairs in a bond chain has at most
\(2|S|+1\) distinct values.  Every periodic subsequence is constant.

### Proof

Contact sets are monotone under inclusion.  Since \(X_i\subseteq X_j\),
\(N_S(X_i)\subseteq N_S(X_j)\), and taking complements in \(S\) gives
the first inclusion in (1.3).  The complements have the reverse
inclusion \(D-X_j\subseteq D-X_i\), giving the second.

Along the chain, a fixed boundary vertex can leave the first defect at
most once and enter the second defect at most once.  Hence there are at
most \(2|S|\) strict changes.  A periodic sequence that is monotone in
opposite coordinates must have equality in both coordinates throughout.

### Corollary 1.2

If \(G\) is \(K_k\)-minor-free and each split in (1.1) lies inside one
of the two full shores, then every \((P_i,Q_i)\) lies in the defect
relation \(\mathcal R_k(G[S])\), but no nontrivial directed cycle in
that relation can be generated by composing the nested splits in their
fixed left-to-right orientation.

Thus the oriented cycles in the \(C_6\dot\cup K_1\) atlas arise from
rotating boundary frames or reversing ownership, not from periodicity
of one nested SPQR path.

## 2. Why defect stabilization is not colour gluing

A constant pair \(P_i=P,Q_i=Q\) records only which boundary vertices
are contacted from the two sides.  It does not record which equality
partitions of an adhesion extend to either side.  Two boundaried graphs
can have identical contact rows and disjoint exact extension families.
Therefore neither Lemma 1.1 nor constancy of a bad defect pair implies a
colouring.

The invariant needed on a laminar decomposition is the decorated profile

\[
 (\text{defect pair},\ \text{exact boundary partition},\
   \text{frame orientation}).                        \tag{2.1}
\]

The next theorem gives the rigorous gluing part once the exact partition
coordinate has been supplied.

## 3. Exact-state gluing on a tree decomposition

### Theorem 3.1 (laminar exact-state gluing)

Let \((T,\{V_t:t\in V(T)\})\) be a tree decomposition of a graph \(G\).
Fix \(r\ge1\).  Suppose that for each \(t\), the graph \(G[V_t]\) has a
proper \(r\)-colouring \(c_t\), and for each tree edge \(tt'\), the two
colourings induce the same equality partition on the adhesion

\[
 X_{tt'}=V_t\cap V_{t'}.                             \tag{3.1}
\]

Then \(G\) is \(r\)-colourable.

### Proof

Root \(T\).  Start with the colouring of the root bag.  Suppose the
union of the bags above an edge \(tt'\), with \(t\) the parent, has
already been coloured and agrees with \(c_t\) on equality classes.
The restrictions of \(c_t,c_{t'}\) to \(X_{tt'}\) have the same
partition.  Map the colour used by \(c_{t'}\) on each nonempty block to
the colour used by the existing colouring on that block.  This partial
bijection between colours extends to a permutation of the \(r\)-colour
palette.  Apply that permutation to \(c_{t'}\), and later to the entire
subtree rooted at \(t'\).  The two colourings now agree pointwise on the
adhesion.

Proceed away from the root.  The running- intersection property of a
tree decomposition makes the assignments to every repeated vertex
consistent, and every graph edge is contained in a bag.  The resulting
global colouring is proper.

### Corollary 3.2

If a laminar family of tight shore separations can be completed to a
tree decomposition in which minor-critical transfer supplies one common
exact partition on each adhesion, the separator outcome of the
balanced-core programme is complete: it colours \(G\).

No anticompleteness beyond that supplied by the tree decomposition is
being assumed, and all portal vertices in an adhesion are coloured on
both incident bags.

## 4. The cycle/web qualification

For a cyclic decomposition in which every overlap is an indicated
consecutive adhesion, pairwise equality of *unlabelled*
partitions is not alone sufficient to run the rooted-tree proof.  After
successively permuting palettes around the cycle, the product
permutation may return a different labelling to the first adhesion.
Call this product the **colour holonomy**.

### Lemma 4.1

A cyclic sequence of bag colourings, covering all graph edges and with
no nonconsecutive bag overlap, glues to an \(r\)-colouring whenever the
induced equality partitions agree on consecutive adhesions and the
colour holonomy fixes the colours used on the closing adhesion.

### Proof

Align palettes successively around the cycle exactly as in Theorem 3.1.
All intermediate adhesions agree pointwise.  The final and initial
colourings agree on the closing adhesion precisely when the product
permutation fixes its used colours.  Under that condition the aligned
bag colourings have a well-defined proper union.

This is the exact-state content missing from a defect-only assertion
that a periodic transition “gives a web”.  A valid balanced-core
dichotomy must either:

1. produce a rooted minor before the defect chain stabilizes;
2. produce a laminar tree of adhesions with the common exact states
   required by Theorem 3.1; or
3. produce a cyclic web together with trivial colour holonomy as in
   Lemma 4.1.

The defect atlas alone proves none of the state or holonomy assertions.
They must come from the one-step deletion/contraction witnesses.
