## 3. The exact atomic `HC_7` obstruction on this route

After the `K_7^vee/Q`-full normalization and the successful shore branch,
the hard local object is:

* a literal seven-cut
  
  \[
                   S=Q\dot\cup Z_D\dot\cup Z_E,
                   \qquad |Q|=3, |Z_D|=|Z_E|=2,
  \]
  
  with two full open sides;
* `Q` is a triangle, and the two gate pairs arise as inverse images of
  capacity-one state warehouses;
* `F=overline{G[S]}` is non-split, has at least four edges, and contains
  an induced `2K_2`, `C_4`, or `C_5`;
* the two closed-side six-colour extension families are disjoint, while
  every independent boundary block has a proper-minor private witness;
* every relevant edge-deletion witness has equal endpoints and Kempe
  saturation, but those witnesses have not yet been converted into

There are two genuinely different static geometries.  The
`C_5 dotunion 2K_1` boundary occurs in the coherent two-apex
icosahedral join.  The missing graph `2K_3 dotunion K_1` gives a sharp
nonrural target-free quotient `K_1 vee K_{2,3,3}`.  Thus quotient geometry
alone cannot force the rural branch; contraction-critical dynamics must
eliminate or reroute the nonrural warehouse.

This is the atomic obstruction for the present exact-gate route, not a
claim that every other formulation of `HC_7` has already been closed.

## 4. The theorem statements which would genuinely close the gap

### A. Protected state-column exchange

> Let `G` be seven-connected, seven-contraction-critical and
> `K_7`-minor-free, and let an exact seven-cut have the triangle-plus-two-
> warehouse form above.  Then the private proper-minor/Kempe witnesses

Outcome (i) feeds the rooted-page/cocycle completion and gives `K_7`;
outcome (ii) colour-glues; outcome (iii) makes the graph two-apex and hence
six-colourable.  A proof would close the exact-gate obstruction.

### B. Global near-`K_7` split-versus-two-apex theorem

> Every seven-connected seven-contraction-critical graph containing a
> `K_7^vee` minor either contains a `K_7` minor or has two vertices whose
> deletion is planar.

Together with the known theorem that every non-six-colourable graph
contains `K_7^vee`, this would prove `HC_7`: the first outcome contradicts
minor exclusion, while the second is six-colourable by four colours on
the planar remainder and two new colours on the apex pair.

The second statement is broader; the first is the more precise current
research target.

## 7. Uniform rooted-model principles extracted from the dynamic gates
