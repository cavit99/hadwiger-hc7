# Exact colour-closure for the reserved-connector shore

## 1. Why the raw multipole analogy is not an identification

Retain the order-six reserved-connector cell.  Thus

\[
 N(v)=\{a,b\}\mathbin{\dot\cup}U,\qquad |U|=5,
\]

where (ab\notin E(G)), (G[U]\cong C_5), and

\[
 S=U\mathbin{\dot\cup}\{w\}.
\]

The two components of (H-S), where (H=G-v), are

\[
 \{a\}\cup D_a,\qquad \{b\}\cup D_b.
\]

The shores (D_a,D_b) are connected and anticomplete, and (D_a) is
full to (L_a=S\cup\{a\}), while (D_b) is full to
(L_b=S\cup\{b\}).

A state of a shore in the counterexample is an equality partition of its
**seven-label** boundary into at most six independent colour classes.
There is no direct bijection between these states and states of a cubic
5-pole.  A 5-pole has three edge colours and encodes four vertex colours
on a facial pentagon.  Here the two omitted labels (w,a) (or (w,b))
are merely full to the shore: they are not complete to it and their two
colours need not be absent from the shore interior.  Therefore deleting
those labels from an arbitrary six-colouring does not leave a four-colouring.

The valid translation below concerns a precisely defined
four-palette **subfamily** of the actual six-colour state space.  No claim
about the other six-colour states is made.

## 2. The actual four-palette subfamily

Let (Omega_4(U)) be the set of equality partitions of (U) induced by
proper four-colourings of the cycle (G[U]).  Colour names are forgotten.
There are ten such states.

For (i\in\{a,b\}), define

\[
 {cal P}(D_i)=
 \left\{\pi\in\Omega_4(U):
 G[D_i\cup U]\text{ has a proper four-colouring with trace }\pi
 \right\}.
 \tag{2.1}
\]

Every (pi\in{cal P}(D_a)) gives an actual six-colouring of
(G[D_a\cup L_a]): use colours (1,2,3,4) on (D_a\cup U), give
(a) colour (5), and give (w) colour (6).  Thus it gives the
seven-label equality state

\[
 \iota_a(\pi)=\pi\mid\{a\}\mid\{w\}.
 \tag{2.2}
\]

This remains proper regardless of the placements of the (a)- and
(w)-portals, because their colours are absent from the whole of
(D_a\cup U).  The same construction defines
(iota_b(\pi)=\pi\mid\{b\}\mid\{w\}).

### Lemma 2.1 (common palette state closes the cell)

If

\[
 {cal P}(D_a)\cap{cal P}(D_b)\ne\varnothing,
 \tag{2.3}
\]

then (G) is six-colourable.

### Proof

Choose four-colourings on (D_a\cup U) and (D_b\cup U) with the same
equality trace on (U).  Permute the four colours on one side so that the
two colourings agree pointwise on (U).  They glue because the shores are
anticomplete.  Give (a,b) the same fifth colour and give (w,v) the
same sixth colour.

The pair (a,b) is independent.  The vertex (w) is not a neighbour of
(v), since (w\notin N(v)).  Every edge from (a,b,w) to a shore or
to (U) has ends in disjoint palettes, and (v) sees only
({a,b\}\cup U).  Hence the resulting colouring is proper. (square)

This is the relevant colour-gluing statement in the actual graph.  It is
stronger than merely finding matching unnamed multipole states: Section 3
proves that the names correspond exactly.

## 3. Exact Tait translation for a simultaneous pentagonal web

Suppose (P=G[D\cup U]) has a plane embedding in a closed disk in which
(G[U]) is the boundary 5-cycle.  Add plane vertices and edges in bounded
faces, if necessary, until every bounded face is a triangle, without
changing the outer 5-cycle, and call the resulting near-triangulation
(T).  Every four-colouring of
(T) is a four-colouring of (P), so

\[
 {cal P}(T)\subseteq{cal P}(D).
 \tag{3.1}
\]

Form a cubic 5-pole (M_T) as follows.  Its vertices are the bounded
triangular faces of (T); an internal edge of (T) gives the usual dual
edge, and each of the five boundary edges gives one semiedge.

### Lemma 3.1 (state-by-state bijection)

There is a bijection

\[
 \Theta:\Omega_4(U)\longrightarrow
 \{\text{admissible states of a labelled cubic 5-pole}\}/S_3
 \tag{3.2}
\]

which maps ({\cal P}(T)) exactly to ({\rm Col}(M_T)).

### Proof

Identify the four vertex colours with the group
(A=\mathbb F_2^2).  If (c) is a proper four-colouring of (T), colour
the dual of a primal edge (xy) by

\[
 \delta(xy)=c(x)+c(y)\in A-\{0\}.
 \tag{3.3}
\]

On a triangular face the three values are the three distinct nonzero
elements of (A), so (3.3) is a Tait colouring of (M_T).  Its semiedge
state is

\[
 \bigl(c(u_i)+c(u_{i+1}):i\in\mathbb Z_5\bigr),
 \tag{3.4}
\]

where (u_0u_1\cdots u_4u_0) is the outer cycle.

Conversely, a Tait colouring gives a nonzero value on every primal edge.
Fix the colour of one primal vertex and integrate these values along
primal paths.  The sum around every triangular face is zero.  Since the
disk is simply connected and its cycle space is generated by its bounded
face boundaries, the integrated colour is well defined.  Adjacent
vertices differ, and this recovers a proper four-colouring of (T).

Translation by an element of (A) changes no edge value.  The group
({\rm GL}(2,2)) permutes the three nonzero values, and every permutation
of the four vertex colours is an affine map of (A):

\[
 AGL(2,2)\cong S_4.
\]

Therefore quotienting vertex colourings by (S_4) and Tait states by
(S_3) gives exactly the equality-state bijection (3.2). (square)

This is the promised exact translation.  It applies to ({\cal P}(T)),
not to the full seven-label six-colour extension family.

## 4. Why the all-crossless web is a simultaneous pentagonal patch

Index the missing-edge cycle on (U) as
(01,12,23,34,40).  Then the present cycle (G[U]) has pentagram order

\[
 0,2,4,1,3.
 \tag{4.1}
\]

For a shore (D), add five artificial terminals, terminal (i) adjacent
to the full portal set (N_D(i)).  A crossing of the five-terminal tuple
in the circular order (4.1) is exactly a linkage for two vertex-disjoint
edges of the missing cycle.  Hence, if all five Moser frames are crossless,
the entire five-terminal tuple is crossless, not merely each of five
unrelated four-terminal societies.

Add edges, without adding vertices, to an edge-maximal supergraph in which
this ordered terminal tuple is still crossless.  In the terminology of
Humeau--Pous, a crossing of an ordered tuple is any two disjoint terminal
paths whose four ends alternate in the tuple order; for five terminals
these are exactly the five Moser frames above.  Their generalized Two
Paths Theorem (Theorem 1.5) therefore makes the maximal supergraph a
5-web with the five terminals on its frame.  An inserted clique behind a
facial triangle has at most those three rib vertices and the two omitted
labels (w,a) (or (w,b)) as external neighbours in the full graph.  That is a cut of
order at most five separating a nonempty inserted part from the opposite
shore, contrary to seven-connectivity.  Thus the web is bare.

Consequently (G[D\cup U]) is a plane pentagonal patch with outer cycle
in the present-edge order (4.1).  This supplies exactly the hypothesis of
Section 3.  In particular, the occurrence-level all-crossless theorem in
`hadwiger_reserved_connector_rank_leaf.md` may be followed by one
simultaneous 5-web, rather than five independently chosen embeddings.

## 5. Bilateral all-crossless closure

The simultaneous nature of Section 4 is stronger than a lower bound on
state sets.

### Theorem 5.1 (two pentagonal webs glue)

The two reserved shores cannot both be all-crossless.

### Proof

Suppose that both shores are all-crossless. By Section 4, for each
(i in {a,b}) the graph

\[
 P_i=G[D_i\cup U]
\]

has a closed-disk embedding whose boundary is the same labelled cycle
(G[U]), in its present-edge pentagram order.

Reverse the orientation of one disk if necessary and identify the two
copies of their boundary cycle vertex by vertex and edge by edge. The
topological union of the two disks is a sphere. All edges of (P_a) lie
in one closed hemisphere and all edges of (P_b) lie in the other. There
are no edges between (D_a) and (D_b), because they are the two open
shores of the separation. Hence this is an embedding of the actual graph

\[
 P_a\cup_{G[U]}P_b=G[D_a\cup D_b\cup U]
 \tag{5.1}
\]

in the sphere. The Four Color Theorem gives one proper four-colouring of
(5.1). Its restrictions to the two hemispheres have the same equality
state on (U). Therefore

\[
 {cal P}(D_a)\cap{cal P}(D_b)\ne\varnothing.
\]

Lemma 2.1 gives a proper six-colouring of (G), a contradiction. (square)

The proof uses neither a choice of triangulations nor a numerical
multipole bound. The crucial input is that Section 4 produces one
simultaneous five-terminal disk on each side. Five separately chosen
four-terminal web embeddings would not justify the spherical gluing.

## 6. A rigorous colour-closed criterion in the actual state space

Fiol and Vilaltella prove that a Tait-colourable cubic 5-pole has at least
three non-equivalent states, and that there are ten admissible states in
total (Lemma 4.1 and the formula for (sigma(5)) in *Some Results on the
Structure of Multipoles in the Study of Snarks*, EJC 22 (2015), P1.45).
Together with Lemma 3.1 this gives the following statement purely in the
actual four-palette subspace.

### Theorem 6.1 (pentagonal palette closure)

If (D) is an all-crossless reserved shore, then

\[
 |{cal P}(D)|\ge3.
 \tag{6.1}
\]

If both (D_a,D_b) are all-crossless and either

\[
 |{cal P}(D_a)|\ge8
 \quad\hbox{or}\quad
 |{cal P}(D_b)|\ge8,
 \tag{6.2}
\]

then the reserved-connector cell is impossible. In the present bilateral
all-crossless setting this is superseded by Theorem 5.1, but the numerical
criterion remains valid for any two separately certified pentagonal
patches.

### Proof

Triangulate each bare pentagonal patch.  The Four Color Theorem makes its
dual 5-pole Tait-colourable, so its at least three states map into
({\cal P}(D)) by (3.1) and Lemma 3.1.  This proves (6.1).

Under (6.2), the two subsets of the ten-element universe
(Omega_4(U)) have orders at least eight and three.  They intersect.
Lemma 2.1 finishes. (square)

More generally, it is enough that one selected triangulation dual be
colour-closed in the literal multipole sense; its state set then meets the
state set of a selected triangulation of the other shore.  Condition (6.2)
is the directly checkable sufficient form.

## 7. Trace-closure in the full six-colour state space

There is a separate exact criterion which uses no planarity.  Let (L)
be either seven-label shore boundary, let (Omega_6(G[L])) be all
partitions of (L) into at most six independent blocks, and for every
nonempty independent (I\subseteq L) put

\[
 C_I=\{\pi\in\Omega_6(G[L]): I\text{ is a block of }\pi\}.
 \tag{7.1}
\]

The full-shore exact-trace lemma says that the outside extension family
({\cal O}) meets every (C_I).

### Proposition 7.1 (exact trace-closed criterion)

Let ({\cal F}\subseteq\Omega_6(G[L])).  The following are equivalent.

1. ({\cal F}) meets every abstract family
   ({\cal O}\subseteq\Omega_6(G[L])) satisfying
   ({\cal O}\cap C_I\ne\varnothing) for every nonempty independent
   (I\subseteq L).
2. (C_I\subseteq{cal F}) for some nonempty independent (I\subseteq L).

### Proof

Condition 2 implies 1 because every admissible ({\cal O}) meets that
cylinder.  Conversely, if no cylinder is contained in ({\cal F}), choose
(pi_I\in C_I-{cal F}) for every (I), and put
({\cal O}=\{\pi_I:I\}).  This family meets every cylinder and is
disjoint from ({\cal F}). (square)

Applied with ({\cal F}={\cal E}_6(G[D\cup L],L)), condition 2 directly
six-colours (G): the actual outside family meets (C_I), so the two
extension families intersect and glue.

Proposition 7.1 also proves a limitation.  Independent-set traces alone
do not justify importing the numerical (8/10) multipole threshold into
the 41-state seven-label space.  The nontrivial (8/10) criterion is valid
only in the palette-separated pentagonal subspace of Sections 2--6.

## 8. Exact remaining gap

The bilateral all-web cell is closed. Therefore any surviving reserved
connector has at least one shore with a crossed Moser frame. The remaining
task is no longer to prove an 8/10 state bound. It is to combine a crossing
on one shore with the all-crossless web, rank-one leaf, or crossing
structure on the other shore so as to obtain either

1. a common four-block portal state from
   `hadwiger_reserved_connector_portal_transfer.md`;
2. an explicit (N(v))-meeting (K_6)-model; or
3. a boundary-extension-preserving internal reduction contradicting
   minor-criticality.

The two omitted labels being merely full is no longer hidden. They are
used safely only as the two reserved colours in Lemma 2.1 and to eliminate
inserted web cliques in Section 4. No multipole statement is applied to
the remaining one-crossed-side cell.
