# Active goal: atomic fully crossed carrier-or-rooted-model theorem

**Status:** research target, not a proved theorem.

## Strategic decision

Keep the exact-seven `(1,2)` programme as the primary engine, but stop
asking an uncoloured web decomposition to transport an exact boundary
colouring state.  The independently audited near-full carrier theorem
shows that this strengthening is unnecessary: once the correct two literal
carriers are found, a named proper-minor contraction manufactures a fresh
packet-demand-at-most-two state, which the two full rich packets reflect.

The active theorem is therefore a geometric rooted-model principle.  It
must return either the carriers needed by the audited list/duty theorem or
five literal rooted bags.  Near-`K_7` model regeneration remains the
independent handoff if a labelled near model appears, but an unlabelled
`K_7^vee` is not a success outcome here.

This goal is the first mandatory milestone of the archived stateful
support-four programme.  Proving it closes a genuine unbounded atomic
family; it does not by itself prove `HC_7`.

## Frozen input

Use the atomic exact-seven separation

\[
 V(G)=A\mathbin{\dot\cup}S\mathbin{\dot\cup}R,
 \qquad S=W\mathbin{\dot\cup}\{u\},\qquad |S|=7,
\]

with the following audited hypotheses.

1. `G` is seven-connected, `K_7`-minor-free, not six-colourable, and every
   proper minor is six-colourable.
2. There is no `A-R` edge; `A` is connected and `S`-full.
3. `zu` is the unique edge from `A` to `u`.
4. `R` contains two disjoint adjacent connected `S`-full packets.
5. The boundary frontier `H=G[S]` is either connected bipartite (a tree or
   a six-cycle with one pendant vertex) or
   `K_{1,3} dotunion K_3`.
6. The root-deletion normalization gives `A-z` connected and `W`-full.
7. The near-full carrier theorem gives two further facts: every clean edge
   of the normalized rooted path/`Y` core closes, and every nonsingleton
   atomic shore `A` is two-connected.

Fix the normalized literal path or subdivided `Y` core `T` with `z` as a
leaf.  The live case has every edge of `T` crossed by a literal `T`-bridge.
No virtual completion edge may be used as a host edge.

## Target theorem

### Atomic fully crossed carrier-or-rooted-`K_5` theorem

Under the frozen input, at least one of the following holds.

1. There are disjoint nonempty connected adjacent sets
   `C_0,C_1 subseteq A`, with `z in C_0`, and a clique reservoir
   `U subseteq S` of order at most one such that the literal contact lists
   on `F=H-U` admit a proper two-colouring using both carrier labels and
   satisfying the audited retained duty.
2. `G[A union S]` contains five pairwise disjoint connected pairwise
   adjacent subgraphs, each containing a different literal member of `S`.

Outcome 1 closes by the audited adaptive clique-reservoir return: contract
the two named carriers, take a six-colouring of that proper minor, reflect
the exact returned state with the two full packets in `R`, align palettes,
and glue.  Outcome 2, together with those two adjacent full packets, is a
literal `K_7` model.

The carriers need not span `A`.  The rooted bags need not be singleton or
prescribed in advance.  No state-pullback, recursive boundary map, or
fixed-pair conclusion is required for this atomic theorem.

## First theorem-level milestone

Prove the target first for a connected bipartite frontier by an
**alternating four-portal decoder**.

1. Use two-connectivity of `A` to choose an `st`-ordering or an ear step
   whose prefix and suffix are literal connected adjacent carriers.
2. Encode a boundary literal by the interval between its first and last
   carrier portal.  At a fixed cut, failure of every allowed singleton
   clique-reservoir return is witnessed by parity-incompatible paths in
   the boundary frontier.
3. Prove the path-hypergraph reduction: a nonreturning cut has two
   vertex-disjoint bad boundary paths, hence four distinct literal portal
   labels.  This is only a certificate; it is not yet a rooted model.
4. Decode those four labels in the host graph.  A literal crossing must
   produce either the two required carriers or five rooted bags.  In the
   crossless branch, use the recursive parallel-composition structure of a
   generalized web to expose the same outcome at a genuine host-side
   composition node.  Added web-completion edges are forbidden.

The immediate base reductions are now:

* the connected-bipartite atom closes whenever `d_{G[A]}(z)<=2`;
* a bare cyclic shore `G[A]=C_n` closes for every `n>=4`;
* therefore a survivor has root degree at least three and genuinely
  noncyclic bridge/ear structure.

These two deductions are separate audited inputs, not substitutes for the
four-portal decoder.  The exceptional `K_{1,3} dotunion K_3` frontier is a
parallel subcase: a list obstruction must be decoded directly as an
`S`-rooted `K_5`, using its untouched triangle.

## Literature boundary

Current primary literature supplies important pieces but not the target.

* Humeau--Pous gives literal disjoint crossings or a same-vertex web
  completion for an arbitrary terminal tuple, and recursively describes
  webs by parallel composition.  It does not preserve carrier labels or
  equality states, and completion edges are not literal host edges.
* Hayashi--Kawarabayashi--Yoo gives a four-terminal
  diamond/`K_{2,3}^+`/`K_5^-` subdivision trichotomy under stronger
  relative-separation and terminal-neighbour hypotheses.  It is a decoder
  candidate only after those hypotheses and the duty labels are verified.
* Norin--Totschnig guarantees a `K_7^vee` minor in every non-six-colourable
  graph and shows the value of rooted `C_5` models in the degree-seven
  Moser cell.  It does not repair the missing edges of a labelled near
  model.
* Kawarabayashi--Yu's knitted theorem operates at connectivity far above
  this seven-connected interface.
* Colorful-minor and rooted-folio theories preserve bounded annotated
  minor data, not exact colour-extension languages.
* Dvorak--Swart shows why abstract boundary-colouring languages alone are
  too flexible; the literal contraction-critical geometry is essential.

## Success and falsification tests

The goal succeeds only when the target theorem is proved and independently
audited, with every contraction set, bag adjacency, reservoir duty, and
packet reflection checked literally.

A counterexample falsifies it only if it satisfies the entire frozen
atomic kernel and has neither an admissible carrier pair nor an
`S`-rooted `K_5`.  Support-only interval examples, graphs lacking
`K_7`-minor-freeness, and geometric bridge hulls containing a literal
`K_7` do not falsify the theorem.

The initial bounded falsifier examined all 70 biconnected NetworkX-atlas
thin-shore shapes of orders three through six and 265 sampled contact
assignments satisfying the relative seven-cut inequalities, deleted-root
fullness, and absence of a rooted near-full carrier pair.  Every sample had
an exactly verified `S`-rooted `K_5`.  This is encouraging support-level
evidence only: the search does not encode the full crossed hull, the rich
shore, global seven-connectivity, `K_7`-minor-freeness, minor-criticality,
or exact attained states.

Terminate the `st`/web mechanism if it generates an unbounded family of
new label states or a genuine transition cycle with no carrier/rooted-model
output.  In that event, retain the target theorem but switch the proof
engine to a minimal-ear or labelled-near-model regeneration argument; do
not expand the boundary census.

## What remains after success

Even a complete proof leaves the nonseparating rich-route alternative,
arbitrary attained demand-three `(1,2)` states, packet vector `(1,1)`,
larger adhesions, and global near-`K_7` rotation coherence.  Those are the
next proof-spine edges.  The present goal is chosen because it closes the
exact atomic obstruction now reached and asks for one reusable rooted-model
principle rather than further labelled enumeration.
