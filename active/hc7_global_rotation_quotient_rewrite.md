# Global rotation quotient and exact-seven rewrite rank

**Status:** proved quotient/rank lemmas and a conditional flat-component
discharge; the unrestricted sink-coherence theorem is open.  Independent
audit is required before any proved part is promoted to `results/`.  This
note does not prove `HC_7`.  It supplies the safe well-founded object for
several local handoffs and states the exact closure hypothesis which those
handoffs must satisfy.

The main point is that the size of a deficient centre is not a rank.  A
single-gate rotation is an involution.  The correct neutral object is its
entire two-bag carrier, while the correct directed object in `S3` is the
packet-one open shore of an oriented actual `(1,2)` cell.

## 1. Literal near-model states

Fix a finite graph `G`.  A **fixed-frame near state** is literal data

\[
             x=(A_x,B_x;F_1,\ldots,F_5),                 \tag{1.1}
\]

where all seven sets are nonempty, connected and pairwise disjoint,
`F_1,...,F_5` are pairwise adjacent, `A_xB_x` is nonempty, `B_x` meets
every `F_i`, and

\[
 D_x=\{i:E(A_x,F_i)=\varnothing\}
\]

has order one or two.  Put

\[
                   C_x=A_x\cup B_x.                     \tag{1.2}
\]

The ordered five-tuple of rows, including their literal vertex sets, is
the **frame** and `C_x` is the **coarse carrier**.

Join two states by a neutral edge when one is obtained from the other by
an audited single-connected-gate rotation.  Thus, in the notation of the
rotation theorem,

\[
 B_x=Z\mathbin{\dot\cup}W,qquad
 A_y=W,qquad B_y=A_x\cup Z,                             \tag{1.3}
\]

and the five `F_i` are unchanged.  Write `x ~_0 y`, and let `~` be the
equivalence relation generated by these neutral edges.

### Lemma 1.1 (the literal quotient invariant)

If `x ~ y`, then `x,y` have the same five literal frame rows and

\[
                             C_x=C_y.                    \tag{1.4}
\]

Hence

\[
 \sigma([x])=(F_1,\ldots,F_5;C_x),qquad
 \rho([x])=|C_x|                                        \tag{1.5}
\]

are well defined on every rotation-equivalence class.

#### Proof

For one edge, (1.3) gives

\[
 A_y\cup B_y=W\cup A_x\cup Z=A_x\cup B_x.
\]

The fixed rows do not move.  Induction along a neutral walk proves the
claim.  \(\square\)

This is the smallest safe quotient.  The centre, donor, missing set, bag
size vector, portal owners and any colouring chosen from a proper-minor
response can all change inside one class.  None of them belongs in
`sigma` without an additional transport theorem.

## 2. Directed receivers and the class rank

A **ranked receiver** is an edge between two *distinct neutral classes*
`Q,Q'` for which a literal class invariant `C(Q) subseteq V(G)` has first
been defined and the construction proves

\[
                         C(Q')\subsetneq C(Q).           \tag{2.1}
\]

For a fixed-frame near class, Lemma 1.1 permits `C(Q)=A union B`.  A
different state type may use a different carrier only after proving that
the carrier is constant on its entire neutral class.  A representative-
local inequality `|L_y|<|L_x|` is not enough: a neutral move at the target
may reset the declared active-set order before the next strict edge.

### Theorem 2.1 (termination modulo rotations)

Let `X_0,X_1,...` be a rewrite sequence in which every step is either a
neutral rotation inside one equivalence class or a ranked receiver, and
quotient all maximal neutral intervals.  The resulting quotient sequence
is finite.  In particular, no directed cycle contains a ranked receiver.

#### Proof

At every quotient step (2.1) strictly decreases the nonnegative integer
`|C(Q)|`.  Neutral rotations have already been collapsed and, by
hypothesis, do not change `C(Q)`.  A strictly descending sequence of
nonnegative integers is finite.  \(\square\)

The theorem does not require the returned equality partition to equal the
source partition.  It requires a rank on *classes*, not on selected
representatives.

## 3. The exact-seven minimum principle

Let `C_12` be the class of all **oriented actual exact-seven cells**

\[
             (L,S,R),\qquad |S|=7,qquad
             (\nu_L,\nu_R)=(1,2),                       \tag{3.1}
\]

with both open shores nonempty.  Orientation is always reset so that `L`
is the packet-one shore.  No equality state is part of membership in
`C_12`.

### Theorem 3.1 (state-free strict `(1,2)` descent)

Assume `C_12` is nonempty and choose a member minimizing `|L|`.  There is
no literal handoff to another member

\[
                  (L',S',R')\in C_12
\]

with `|L'|<|L|`.  This remains true if the new cell has a different
boundary graph, a different legally attained exact partition, or a
different rich-shore packet realization.

#### Proof

The class is finite because `G` is finite.  A receiver satisfying (3.1)
is another candidate in the same minimization.  The inequality contradicts
the choice of `L`.  None of the other displayed data enters the minimizing
coordinate.  \(\square\)

### Closure warning

Theorem 3.1 cannot be applied after silently restricting the source to the
paired-triangle, width-two, connected-rich, Moser, twin-seam, or any other
special subclass.  It removes state preservation in exactly either of the
following two situations.

1. The proof starts with a global `|L|`-minimum of `C_12` and derives the
   local hypotheses without restricting the equality state; or
2. a smaller subclass `D subseteq C_12` is explicitly proved closed under
   the proposed handoff, and the source was chosen `|L|`-minimum in `D`.

If the receiver leaves the class on which the local theorem is valid, its
smaller shore is progress but not a contradiction.  One must either give a
decoder valid for every state subsequently reached, or supply a separate
ranked `S1/S4` handoff.  This is the exact orientation/subclass caveat in
the current twin-seam packet-transfer output.

## 4. Flat response height inside one reversible class

There is a second, independent way to discharge a rotation class.  It is
applicable only to two-hole overlap moves whose proper-minor responses
carry one coherent rural order.

Let `Q` be one rotation-equivalence class of two-hole states.  A directed
response arc

\[
 x\longrightarrow y,qquad
 D_x=\{a,b\},\quad D_y=\{a,c\},                         \tag{4.1}
\]

records the comparison `b < c`.  Call a family of response arcs **flat**
when one linear order `prec` of `[5]` realizes every recorded comparison.
The audited fixed-root and set-root rural-order theorems produce exactly
this hypothesis when all absent private linkages live in one fixed
connector society with one fixed ordered root pair or root blocks.

For a flat family put

\[
 h_\prec(x)=\operatorname{rk}_\prec(a)+
             \operatorname{rk}_\prec(b)                 \tag{4.2}
\]

when `D_x={a,b}`.

### Theorem 4.1 (flat sink-component discharge)

Suppose that, for every `x in Q`, the local decoder returns at least one
of:

1. a literal `K_7`, a common exact state and six-colouring, or a valid
   fixed-pair terminal;
2. a ranked receiver leaving `Q`; or
3. a flat response arc `x -> y` inside `Q` of the form (4.1).

Then some representative of `Q` has outcome 1 or 2.  In particular, a
flat reversible component cannot be a nonterminal sink of the quotient
rewrite system.

#### Proof

Choose `x in Q` maximizing `h_prec(x)`.  If the decoder returned an
internal arc (4.1), flatness gives

\[
 \operatorname{rk}_\prec(b)<\operatorname{rk}_\prec(c),
 \qquad h_\prec(y)>h_\prec(x),
\]

contrary to maximality.  Hence the decoder at `x` returns outcome 1 or
2.  \(\square\)

Equivalently, the global rewrite measure is lexicographic: first the
class-invariant carrier order from Section 2, then the negative of the
finite rural height inside the current neutral component.  One never
orients a bare rotation.  One chooses an extremal representative of its
whole reversible class and orients only the proper-minor response.

### Corollary 4.2 (active rural composition)

Suppose in addition that the flat responses occur in the active rural
chain of the audited active-root theorem, the chain covers `G-{p,q}` for
two fixed literal vertices, and every first two-coordinate loss is decoded
as a literal `K_7`, a common exact state, or a ranked receiver.  Then the
chain yields one of those three outcomes or

\[
                         G-\{p,q\}\text{ is planar}.     \tag{4.3}
\]

Thus every sink in this active-overlap-saturated subsystem is terminal or
has a strict quotient exit.

#### Proof

Apply the active rural-chain trichotomy.  An incompatible annular order
gives the labelled `K_7`; compatible orders glue to (4.3); and the first
failed interface is, by hypothesis, decoded to a `K_7`, common state, or
ranked receiver.  Theorem 4.1 removes a residual directed cycle inside a
flat rotation class.  \(\square\)

This corollary is a composition theorem, not the missing local decoder.
Its purpose is to show that once the atomic two-coordinate/twin-seam
exchange produces a truly ranked receiver, no further global rotation
potential is needed in this subsystem.

## 5. Falsification boundary

The class-invariance clause in Section 2 is essential.  The audited
two-apex tube contains a reduced three-rotation cycle on one fixed frame
whose centre orders are `1,4,1` while the coarse carrier is constant.
More abstractly, two neutral classes containing representatives of orders
`4,1` and `3,5` admit strict-looking arrows `4 -> 3` and `5 -> 4`, yet
neutral resets close a cycle.  The literal certificate and verifier are in
`../barriers/hc7_global_invariant_rotation_triangle.md`.  Thus a local
shore/centre decrease is not a quotient rank unless neutral equivalence
preserves the shore being compared.

The flat order in Theorem 4.1 is essential.  Three different rural
connectors may separately realize

\[
                 2<3,\qquad 3<4,\qquad 4<2,             \tag{5.1}
\]

on the three overlap moves

\[
 \{1,2\}\to\{1,3\}\to\{1,4\}\to\{1,2\}.              \tag{5.2}
\]

Every individual comparison extends to a linear order, but their union
does not.  A small verifier is recorded in
`../barriers/hc7_rotation_local_orders_do_not_globalize_verify.py`.
Geometrically, each comparison can be drawn in its own path/disk page.
Therefore local rurality, pair overlap and exact reversibility do not
produce a height unless the literal root/connector transport theorem puts
the comparisons in one society.

Similarly, Theorem 3.1 does not make an arbitrary strict lobe recursive:
the receiver must remain in the globally minimized class.  These are the
two genuine global compatibility obligations left after quotienting the
rotations.

## 6. Consequence for the current atomic theorem

The target outcome “strict, noncycling exact-seven `S1/S4` handoff” can
now be certified in either of two precise ways.

* **Exact-seven route:** choose the source globally minimal among all
  actual oriented `(1,2)` cells and exhibit another actual oriented
  `(1,2)` receiver with a smaller packet-one shore.  This is the separate
  minimum principle of Section 3, not an extension of a near-model
  rotation rank.
* **Near-model route:** stay within one reversible coarse-carrier class,
  transport all response comparisons into one fixed rural order, and use
  the extremal representative of Theorem 4.1; any nonterminal response
  must then leave by a support-decreasing receiver.

A naked high-demand lobe, a changed equality partition, or a one-/two-hole
model on a different frame satisfies neither bullet by itself.

The remaining global statement is not hidden by this formalism: a sink
neutral component whose response comparisons change connector/root society
still needs a proof of one common exact state, a strict class exit, or one
actual pair meeting every `K_5` model.  The rotation quotient identifies
that obligation; it does not prove it.
